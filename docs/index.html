The Holographic Librarian

A glowing companion that helps you find your next book — and a film to match.

What it is

The Holographic Librarian is a small interactive installation that greets readers with a glowing, floating figure. Its sensors notice when someone steps close, and can tell when a book is placed nearby. Tell it what you enjoy — space, history, romance — and the librarian speaks back, suggesting a book and a film to match your mood and the room around you. Everything runs offline, with no internet and no cameras recording you. Designed for libraries, bookshops and study spaces, it turns the familiar question of “what should I read next?” into a small, playful conversation.

Try it in three steps

1. Step up

Walk up to the installation. The librarian senses you approach and comes to life.

2. Say what you like

Tell it a topic — space, history, romance. It listens right there on the device. You can also place a book on the desk and it will recognise it.

3. Get a recommendation

It suggests a book and a film to match your mood and the room — and reads them aloud.

Behind the scenes

● Completely offline

No internet needed — the speech, the recommendations and the voice all run on the device itself.

● Private by design

No cameras record you and nothing is sent to the cloud. It only reacts to the moment.

● Feels the room

Small sensors read distance, temperature and humidity to notice who is near and set the mood.

● A real hologram trick

The floating figure is a bright screen reflected onto angled glass — the classic “Pepper’s Ghost” illusion.
